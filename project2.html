Introduction

The Michigan Alliance of TimeBanks is an umbrella organization linking and supporting TimeBanks across the state of Michigan. For every hour people spend doing something for someone in their community they earn one hour. Then they have an hour to spend on having someone to do something for them. I worked on both UX research and design of this project. I worked on the interview protocols, affinity wall analysis, wireframing and redesigned several pages.

  1. Existing members of TimeBanks: People who are already involved in one of more local TimeBanks. They may be new or long-time members.
  2. Potential members of TimeBanks: People who demonstrate an interest in volunteering and/or community building. They may or may not already be familiar with and interested in TimeBanks.

How might we help current and potential members find information regarding TimeBanks through an accessible interface with clear navigation?

Interviews

We conducted 4 interviews; 2 with current members and 2 with potential members to understand different perspectives into the problem statement. Goal of these interview sessions was to gain insights on what potential members look for in the TimeBanks website and what information current members acquire from the present website.

  1. What information do you need regularly / frequently if you are an existing member and how are you currently acquiring them?
  2. What do potential members know about TimeBanks? What’s the most important information that a potential member would like to acquire about TimeBanks, if any?

User Persona

Based on the interviews and analysis we conducted, we designed two personas one for existing members and the other for potential members. We mapped out our key findings from the interviews and personas in the form of empathy maps for both potential and current members analyzing what they think, feel, do and say.

User Journey Map

Since the primary goal of the website was to help potential members of TimeBanks navigate through the website with ease, we set up user journey maps to encapsulate their behavior, sentiment and pain points through different stages of using the app.
